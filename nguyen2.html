Name: Thảo
Some people believe that economic growth is the only way to end poverty and hunger in the world, while others say that it can cause damage to the environment and should be stopped. In my opinion, economic development should not be completely stopped, but it must be carefully managed.
On the one hand, economic growth plays a crucial role in reducing poverty and hunger. This development can reduce income inequality by promoting economic opportunities. As a result, when new industries are found, more jobs will be created. According to this expansion of the economy, the unemployment rate will also decrease. Many low-wage workers can become a breadwinner with a stable income and support their family to meet basic living expenses. In addition, this economic progress also reduces poverty and improves living standards. Many people can be lifted out of poverty when the food shortage decreases and the national income increases. According to this, there will be more investment funds for healthcare, education, and social welfare, so that living standards and the quality of life can be more and more improved.
On the other hand, uncontrolled economic growth causes serious environmental damage. Economic growth can contribute to environmental pollution in many ways. When industries develop too quickly, factories produce large amounts of waste and harmful gases. These industrial activities release toxic emissions into the air, rivers and soil, which cause serious air and water pollution. As a result, the natural environment is damaged and people's health may be affected. The increase of pollution also leads to global warming and climate change. Additionally, A large amount of demand for land, water and energy leads to deforestation and causes habitat destruction. Therefore, natural resources are becoming increasingly depleted.
In conclusion, rather than pursuing economic growth at all costs, governments should focus on sustainable development to balance economic progress with environmental protection.
